Platform Engineer

I'm Nathan, a platform engineer with lots of thoughts on automation and platform design. I started my career as a cloud engineer with no prior experience. It was a challenge learning the fundamentals on the job, but with time, interest and a strong support network I've since found my place in the platform engineering and Kubernetes space. Coming into a fast-moving field fresh and getting hands-on with different solutions to the same problems has really shaped how I think: it's pushed me to think hard about how my solutions will hold up over time and how I can make simple solutions that are easy for the next person to pick up.

Whenever I build automation or design a process, three things drive the decisions: simplicity, maintainability, and scalability. I'm always asking how hard a solution will be to extend or change later and whether it'll actually scale. If it's a process, I want it automated and repeatable enough so that running it again is easy, or at least well documented. If it's a platform, I want it self-serve so consumers move at their own pace and the platform team isn't drowning in support. Lastly, I build for the version of me who comes back in six months having forgotten all the details, because that person should be able to easily enhance it without re-learning everything.

The writing is about the problems I've actually worked through and my opinion on a good approach for tackling them. I'll share code, designs and my thoughts in hopes that someone (or future me) can learn from them.
